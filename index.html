21st Century Teaching & Learning

Timeline of how teachers and students evolved throughout the decades. From the 1960's to the present.​ 

 1960's

TEACHERS

  • All-in-one teacher: All subjects in one section were all taught by a single teacher. 
  • They used chalk and blackboard. As well as television, radio, and overhead projectors.
  •  Teachers used to spend their entire day talking, which meant student most of their day passively sitting and listening.       
  • In the 1960s this was very much 'talk and chalk' education, with the teacher at the front of the class and the children sitting at desks facing the board. Reading, writing and arithmetic (the Three 'R's) were very important, as was learning by rote. Neat hand writing was seen as very important and practiced daily.

STUDENTS

  • The private – The learners during the 1960s can be somewhat compared to the lowest rank of a soldier, in terms of their roles and responsibilities. 
  • Education during this time is completely objective, wherein learning the subject matter is the most important.
  • Printed textbooks -  guide the teaching and learning of the curriculum in a particular subject. 
  • Picture Books -  illustrations shown alongside text offer invaluable tools to help younger learners build understanding, fluency, vocabulary and other foundational literacy skills

 1970's

  • The Disciplinarian – These were the teachers that would hit your hand with a ruler as a means of disciplining the student. This was normalized in the 70s but not so much today.
  • Cassette recorders - Listening to tapes provides students information not only about vocabulary and grammar, but also about pronunciation, intonation, rhythm, stress, and pitch
  • Photographs - used for visual representation and explaining complex concepts
  • Teachers had open classrooms, this setting emphasizes learning through small-group and individual instruction to encourage collaboration and active learning, less government involvement in education. 
  • The most significant test-score declines were found among high-school students. The emphasis of the New Society is on moral values, relevance, proper methods of teaching, retraining of teachers, vocational and technical education, bilingualism, national consciousness and cultural values.

 1980's

STUDENTS

  • Computer - used in a way that was intended to individualize instruction. This method became known as computer-assisted instruction (CAI).
  •  It was during this period that drilling and practice programs were first developed for exclusive classroom use. 
  • Integrated system of education covering both formal and non-formal education at all levels.  
  • Reform of teacher education and of the teaching profession became a key item on the agenda of the 80's
  • Teacher’s role is as a giver of knowledge. Teachers share knowledge with students on a particular subject, through lessons that build on their prior knowledge and moves them toward a deeper understanding of the subject.

 2000's

  • Gives challenging tasks regularly. Follow orders and work hard and you will be ok.
  • Learning management systems - enable teachers and students to collaborate and communicate online in real-time across geography distance.
  • Interactive whiteboards - easily show graphics, photos, or other images to a class and write or draw directly on them. Record and save presented materials for teachers and students to review at a later time.
  • Teachers were a proprietary multimedia software shell that, once loaded with intellectual material such as the contents of a book and lecture, allows a student to interactively control the viewing of that book and lecture.
  • Traditional teaching was the long-established teaching style in Philippine education system wherein the students are passive in terms of participating in class, due to they only have to seat quietly on their places and listen to the teachers' didactic method 
  • Collaborators – These are learners that collaborate with peers to accomplish a certain task or to solve a problem and come up with a solution.
  •  Competitors – These are learners that are motivated to learn for the sake of rankings, not for the sake of learning. 
  • Online educational games - such as Funbrain, increase learners' grammar and vocabulary as well as provide models of appropriate learning practices. Digital textbooks - learners used it for convenience in reading since it is downloadable, making the content available offline, and viewable across multiple electronic devices. Digital textbooks also contain interactive resources that are not found in printed educational materials.

Students

  • Self-learners – Self-learning is where the learner is both the learner and the teacher. 
  • Self-paced learners – These are learners that learn most comfortably in the their own pace and is not being pressured to finish a task at a given time.
